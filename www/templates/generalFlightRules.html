CANADIAN AVIATION REGULATION COMPLIANCE – GENERAL FLIGHT RULES

These rules apply to all UAV operations in Canada. Please read them carefully and acknowledge at the end of the list that your UAV operation does not violate any of these rules.
Must Do
UAV must have name, address, and telephone number of operator visible on aircraft. Determine that the aircraft is fit for flight before takeoff. This includes the pilot deeming it serviceable, and maintenance of all components has been completed and kept up to date in accordance with manufacturer’s instructions Notify the appropriate air traffic control as soon as a UAV is not under pilot’s control or enters or is about to enter controlled airspace. Give way to manned aircraft at all times. Keep the UAV within visual line of sight at all times. Visual observers must communicate with the pilot in timely and reliable way, relaying information on hazards and air traffic. A person can’t be an observer for more than one UAV at a time. An observer cannot perform duties from a moving vehicle without an SFOC. Stop operations the moment aviation safety or the safety of a person or property is threatened. Before take-off or landing ensure the site for take-off/landing is suitable and there is not risk of collision with anything. Prior to operations, the pilot must be familiar with aeronautical charts, NOTAMs, and CFS for the area being flown in. Prior to operations, the pilot must know weather information relevant to their flight. Prior to operations, the pilot must ensure there is enough fuel or energy to complete the flight. Prior to operations, the pilot must ensure each crew member knows their duties during operations as well as location and use of emergency equipment. All crew members must comply with the pilot’s instructions at all times. Operations must be ceased if any of the listed incidents or accidents occur: injuries to a person, contact with a person, animal, vehicle, vessel, building, structure, damage to the UAV, the UAV leaves lateral or altitude boundaries, collision or less of separation (getting too close to) another aircraft, UAV flies away or becomes uncontrollable, any other incident requiring a Canadian Aviation Daily Occurrence Report Prior to operations, pilot must confirm no radio interference is likely. If take-off occurs from an altitude about 10,000 feet ASL all crew members must use oxygen during operations. Manufacturer’s operation manual must be immediately available to all crew members during operation. Operation in transponder airspace is only allowed if a) the UAV has a transponder and pressure-altitude reporting equipment or b) an air traffic control unit allows permission A minimum of $100,000 in liability insurance must be held covering each person involved.
Must Not Do
UAV cannot leave Canadian Domestic Airspace. Do not operate at a lower altitude than allows the aircraft to glide clear of persons or property on the ground in case of emergency landing. Do not operate in close proximity to other aircraft as to create risk of collision. Do not operate in a reckless or negligent fashion. Do not carry living creatures on board a UAV. Do not operate a UAV within 12 hours of consuming an alcoholic beverage, while under the influence of alcohol or drugs, or while impaired. Do not use explosive, corrosive, flammable, biohazardous, or light-emitting payloads without an SFOC. Do not use a portable electronic device near a control station if it may interfere with the control station. Do not operate a UAV unless in accordance with manufacturer’s operating limitations. Do not operate a UAV from a moving vehicle without an SFOC. Do not operate if radio emissions from the aircraft will activate wireless triggers for special effects/fireworks. Do not operate if the pilot cannot take immediate control of the aircraft at any time. Do not use a first-person view device unless a visual observer is also watching the aircraft. Do not hand over control to another pilot or transfer control to another control station without an SFOC. Do not operate more than one UAV at a time. Do not use a flight termination system if it will endanger aircraft, people, or property. Don’t operate a UAV with an ELT on board. Do not use an aircraft that allows flight controls to be lock unless they can’t be locked during flight and warn the pilot whenever flight lock is engaged. Do not operate in aviation events without an SFOC.
